Research on Elevator Height Estimation using Accelerometers

1. Introduction and Principle:
Estimating vertical distance using an accelerometer involves numerical double integration (acceleration -> velocity -> position). Gravity bias must be removed.

2. Challenges:
- Drift: Even tiny sensor noise or bias estimation errors accumulate exponentially during double integration.
- Noise: External vibrations and sensor inaccuracies.

3. Advanced Alternatives:
- ZUPT (Zero Velocity Update): Detecting when the elevator has stopped to reset velocity to zero and correct the previous integrated velocity drift backward.
- Kalman Filtering: Fusing state transitions to maintain constraints (like $v=0$ when $a \approx 0$).
